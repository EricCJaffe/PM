# Department Playbook — Discovery Intake Form (Business)

> Use this form during discovery to assess each department against the Foundation Stone methodology.
> Complete one per department. The 6 pillars (Vision, People, Data, Processes, Meetings, Issues) are assessed for each.
> Answers feed into gap analysis, process mapping, KPIs, and automation opportunity scoring.

---

## Pre-Discovery: Organization Overview

Complete once per engagement (not per department).

### Mission & Identity
- [ ] What is the company's mission statement?
- [ ] What are the core values? Are they documented and visible?
- [ ] What is the 3-year vision? 1-year goals?
- [ ] Who are the primary customers/clients?
- [ ] What makes this company different from competitors?

### Organizational Structure
- [ ] Provide or sketch the org chart
- [ ] How many employees? Full-time vs part-time vs contractors?
- [ ] What departments exist? Who leads each one?
- [ ] How are decisions made? (top-down, consensus, delegated?)
- [ ] Who is the primary decision-maker for this engagement?

### Current State
- [ ] What tools/software does the organization use? (list all)
- [ ] What is working well today?
- [ ] What are the top 3 frustrations or bottlenecks?
- [ ] Has the organization gone through any recent changes? (leadership, growth, restructuring)
- [ ] What has been tried before that didn't work?

### Layer 1: Mission Alignment (VISION)

*How well does this department's work connect to the organization's mission and goals?*

- [ ] What is this department's primary purpose?
- [ ] How does this department contribute to the company's mission?
- [ ] What are this department's top 3 goals for the year?
- [ ] Are these goals written down and tracked?
- [ ] How does the department define success?
- [ ] What would "excellent" look like for this department in 12 months?

### Layer 2: Success Metrics (DATA)

*What numbers does this department track, and how?*

- [ ] What KPIs or metrics does this department currently track?
- [ ] How are they measured? (spreadsheet, dashboard, software, gut feel?)
- [ ] How often are metrics reviewed? By whom?
- [ ] What metrics should be tracked but aren't?
- [ ] Is there a scorecard or dashboard? If yes, who maintains it?
- [ ] What data lives in people's heads that should be in a system?

### Layer 3: People & Organization (PEOPLE)

*Who does what, and is it clear?*

- [ ] Does each person have a written job description?
- [ ] Are roles and responsibilities clear, or is there overlap/confusion?
- [ ] Is there an accountability chart? (who owns what outcomes)
- [ ] How are new team members onboarded?
- [ ] What training or development exists?
- [ ] Are there any staffing gaps or capacity issues?
- [ ] Who is the "go-to" person when something breaks?

### Layer 4: Communication (MEETINGS)

*How does information flow within and across departments?*

- [ ] What recurring meetings does this department have? (daily, weekly, monthly, quarterly)
- [ ] What is the agenda/format for each meeting?
- [ ] How long are meetings? Are they effective?
- [ ] What communication tools are used? (email, Slack, Teams, text, in-person)
- [ ] How does this department communicate with other departments?
- [ ] Is there a weekly team meeting with a consistent agenda?
- [ ] Is there a quarterly planning or review meeting?
- [ ] Where do things fall through the cracks in communication?

### Layer 5: Processes (PROCESSES)

*How is work actually done?*

- [ ] What are the top 5-10 recurring processes in this department?
- [ ] Are any of these documented? (SOPs, checklists, playbooks)
- [ ] What is the most manual or tedious task?
- [ ] What task takes the most time each week?
- [ ] Are there handoffs to other departments? How do they work?
- [ ] What happens when the key person is out sick or on vacation?
- [ ] What processes are paper-based or done in spreadsheets that could be digital?
- [ ] What approval workflows exist? Are they bottlenecks?

### Layer 6: Pain Points (ISSUES)

*What's broken, frustrating, or costing time/money?*

- [ ] What are the top 3 frustrations in this department?
- [ ] What tasks do people dread doing?
- [ ] Where do errors or rework happen most often?
- [ ] What takes too long?
- [ ] Are there any compliance or risk issues?
- [ ] What problems keep coming back even after being "fixed"?
- [ ] If you had a magic wand, what one thing would you change?

### Layer 7: Automation Opportunities (PROCESSES + DATA)

*What could technology or AI improve?*

- [ ] Which repetitive tasks could be automated?
- [ ] Are there data entry tasks that could be eliminated?
- [ ] What reports are created manually that could be auto-generated?
- [ ] Are there notifications or reminders that should be automatic?
- [ ] What integrations between tools are missing?
- [ ] What information do people have to look up in multiple places?
- [ ] Is there any AI opportunity? (content generation, data analysis, customer service)
