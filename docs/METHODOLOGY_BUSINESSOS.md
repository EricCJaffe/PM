# BusinessOS Discovery — Complete Methodology

> Foundation Stone Advisors' structured framework for transforming business operations from tribal knowledge to documented, measurable, repeatable systems.

---

## Overview

BusinessOS Discovery is an 11-phase engagement framework that takes a company from current state to a fully documented operational playbook. Every department is assessed across **6 pillars**, gaps are identified and scored, automation opportunities are ranked, and a master company playbook is produced.

**Duration:** 8-16 weeks depending on org size
**Deliverables:** Department playbooks, master company playbook, automation roadmap, KPI scorecards

---

## The 6 Pillars

Every department is assessed against these 6 operational pillars:

| Pillar | Focus | Key Questions |
|--------|-------|---------------|
| **Vision** | Mission alignment, goals, success definition | Does this department know why it exists and where it's going? |
| **People** | Roles, job descriptions, accountability, onboarding | Does everyone know what they own? |
| **Data** | KPIs, scorecards, dashboards, metrics | What numbers tell you if this department is healthy? |
| **Processes** | SOPs, workflows, handoffs, approvals, triggers | How does work actually get done day-to-day? |
| **Meetings** | Rhythms, cadences, communication tools, channels | How does information flow within and across teams? |
| **Issues** | Pain points, recurring problems, bottlenecks, risks | What's broken and what keeps coming back? |

---

## Pillar Maturity Scoring Rubric

Each pillar is scored 1-5 per department:

| Score | Level | Description |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| **1** | Non-Existent | Nothing exists. No documentation, no tracking, no structure. |
| **2** | Ad-Hoc | Informal. Lives in someone's head. Done differently each time. |
| **3** | Partial | Partially documented. Inconsistently followed. Some people know, some don't. |
| **4** | Documented | Written down. Mostly followed. Some gaps in execution or review. |
| **5** | Optimized | Fully documented, consistently executed, regularly reviewed and improved. |

### Scoring Guidelines

**Vision (1-5):**
- 1: No mission statement or goals exist
- 2: Leadership has informal goals but nothing written
- 3: Goals exist on paper but aren't tracked or communicated
- 4: Goals are documented, tracked quarterly, shared with team
- 5: Goals cascade from org mission → department → individual, reviewed regularly

**People (1-5):**
- 1: No job descriptions, no accountability chart
- 2: Informal roles, verbal agreements, overlap everywhere
- 3: Some job descriptions exist but are outdated or incomplete
- 4: Current job descriptions, accountability chart, onboarding exists
- 5: Clear roles, development plans, succession planning, regular reviews

**Data (1-5):**
- 1: No metrics tracked, decisions based on gut feel
- 2: A few numbers tracked in spreadsheets, reviewed sporadically
- 3: KPIs defined but not consistently measured or reviewed
- 4: Dashboard exists, metrics reviewed weekly/monthly, targets set
- 5: Real-time scorecard, proactive alerts, data-driven decision culture

**Processes (1-5):**
- 1: No SOPs, no checklists, nothing documented
- 2: Key person knows how things work, but it's all verbal
- 3: Some processes documented but not consistently followed
- 4: Core processes have SOPs, handoffs defined, mostly followed
- 5: Full process library, triggers automated, regularly audited and improved

**Meetings (1-5):**
- 1: No regular meetings, communication is random
- 2: Meetings happen but no agenda, no follow-up, inconsistent
- 3: Weekly meetings exist but no standard format, some fall through cracks
- 4: Consistent rhythms (daily/weekly/quarterly), agendas, action items tracked
- 5: Meeting cadence optimized, cross-dept sync, decisions documented and followed up

**Issues (1-5):**
- 1: Problems are ignored or handled reactively with no tracking
- 2: Issues discussed informally but no system to track resolution
- 3: Some issues tracked but many recur, root causes not addressed
- 4: Issue tracking system exists, most get resolved, some root cause analysis
- 5: Structured IDS (Identify, Discuss, Solve), root cause analysis, issues rarely recur

## The 11 Phases

### Phase 0: Executive Commitment & Alignment
**Goal:** Secure leadership buy-in and define engagement objectives.

**Tasks:**
- [ ] Identify executive sponsor
- [ ] Define objectives for the discovery process
- [ ] Align leadership team on goals and expectations
- [ ] Secure commitment from key stakeholders

**Output:** Signed engagement letter, agreed objectives, executive sponsor identified

---

### Phase 1: Vision Alignment
**Goal:** Ensure the organization's mission, vision, and values are clear and current.

**Tasks:**
- [ ] Review current mission & vision statements
- [ ] Conduct vision alignment workshop
- [ ] Draft updated vision document
- [ ] Communicate vision to the organization

**Output:** Updated vision document, alignment across leadership

---

### Phase 2: Leadership Assessment
**Goal:** Map the leadership team, assess strengths, identify gaps.

**Tasks:**
- [ ] Inventory current leaders & roles
- [ ] Assess leadership strengths & competencies
- [ ] Identify leadership gaps
- [ ] Create leadership development plan

**Output:** Leadership assessment report, development plan

---

### Phase 3: Quote-to-Cash Process Discovery
**Goal:** Map the complete revenue cycle from lead to payment.

**Tasks:**
- [ ] Map lead generation process & channels
- [ ] Document lead qualification process (BANT, scoring)
- [ ] Review proposal & quoting workflow
- [ ] Assess negotiation, contract, and close process
- [ ] Map order fulfillment & service delivery workflow
- [ ] Review invoicing process & billing accuracy
- [ ] Assess collections and accounts receivable
- [ ] Identify gaps and bottlenecks in the revenue cycle

**Output:** Revenue cycle map, gap analysis, bottleneck identification

---

### Phase 4: Department Discovery
**Goal:** Structured 7-layer analysis of every department.

**Discovery Layers:** Strategy, Vision, People, Data, Process, Meetings, Issues

**Tasks:**
- [ ] List all departments
- [ ] Assign department discovery leads
- [ ] Run discovery sessions per department
- [ ] Document department findings
- [ ] Identify cross-department issues

**Output:** Per-department 7-layer assessment, cross-department issue register

---

### Phase 5: Dept — Sales
**Goal:** Deep dive into sales pipeline, CRM, team structure.

**Tasks:**
- [ ] Review sales pipeline & CRM usage
- [ ] Assess sales team structure & capacity
- [ ] Document current sales process
- [ ] Identify gaps and improvement areas

---

### Phase 6: Dept — Operations
**Goal:** Map operational workflows, assess tools, find bottlenecks.

**Tasks:**
- [ ] Map core operational workflows
- [ ] Assess tools & systems in use
- [ ] Identify operational bottlenecks
- [ ] Document findings & recommendations

---

### Phase 7: Dept — Finance, People, CS & Other
**Goal:** Assess remaining departments.

**Tasks:**
- [ ] Finance: review AP, AR, budgeting & reporting
- [ ] People/HR: assess hiring, onboarding, retention
- [ ] Customer Service: review support workflows & SLAs
- [ ] Procurement: assess vendor management & sourcing
- [ ] Document findings for remaining departments (IT, Marketing, Legal, Manufacturing)

---

### Phase 8: Gap Analysis & Prioritization
**Goal:** Compile all findings, score gaps, prioritize by impact.

**Tasks:**
- [ ] Compile all discovery data
- [ ] Identify critical gaps
- [ ] Prioritize gaps by impact & urgency
- [ ] Present findings to leadership

**Output:** Gap analysis matrix, priority rankings, leadership presentation

---

### Phase 9: Roadmap Creation
**Goal:** Build the implementation plan with quick wins and long-term milestones.

**Tasks:**
- [ ] Define quick wins (30-day actions)
- [ ] Plan medium-term goals (90 days)
- [ ] Set long-term vision milestones
- [ ] Assign owners for each roadmap item

**Output:** Quarterly roadmap, assigned owners, milestone dates

---

### Phase 10: Enable, Empower, Launch
**Goal:** Transfer knowledge, delegate authority, ensure sustainability.

**Tasks:**
- [ ] Develop training materials
- [ ] Conduct enablement sessions
- [ ] Delegate authority & decision rights
- [ ] Schedule follow-up reviews

**Output:** Training materials, delegation framework, review cadence

## Automation Opportunity Scoring

Each automation opportunity identified during discovery is scored on three dimensions:

| Dimension | Scale | What It Measures |
|-----------|-------|-----------------|
| **Feasibility** | 1-5 | How practical is this to implement? (technology readiness, data availability, complexity) |
| **Impact** | 1-5 | How much value does this create? (time saved, cost reduced, errors eliminated) |
| **Priority** | Calculated | (Feasibility + Impact) × business alignment weight |

### ROI Metrics Captured
- **Estimated time saved** (hours per week/month)
- **Estimated cost savings** (dollars per month)
- **Estimated error reduction** (percentage improvement)
- **Implementation effort** (hours to build)

### Opportunity Status Flow
```
identified → ranked → client-approved → in-progress → implemented
                   ↘ declined
                   ↘ deferred
```

## Department Playbook Structure

Each department produces an 8-section standardized playbook:

1. **Department Overview** — Mission, purpose, org connection
2. **Organizational Chart & Roles** — Team structure, job descriptions, accountability
3. **Key Metrics & Scorecard** — KPIs, targets, review frequency
4. **Meeting Rhythms & Communication** — Recurring meetings, tools, cross-dept coordination
5. **Core Processes & SOPs** — Documented workflows, handoffs, triggers
6. **Known Issues & Resolution Plan** — Pain points, root causes, proposed fixes
7. **Automation Opportunities** — Ranked by ROI, with implementation estimates
8. **Action Plan & Quick Wins** — Prioritized immediate actions

---

## Master Company Playbook

All department playbooks are compiled into a single **Master Company Playbook** that includes:

- Table of contents
- All department playbook sections
- Organization-wide automation opportunities (aggregated and ranked)
- Implementation roadmap
- ROI projections

This becomes the **single source of truth** for how the organization operates.
