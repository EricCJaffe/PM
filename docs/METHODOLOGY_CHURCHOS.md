# ChurchOS Discovery — Complete Methodology

> Foundation Stone Advisors' structured framework for transforming church and ministry operations from tribal knowledge to documented, measurable, reproducible systems — while keeping the mission at the center.

---

## Overview

ChurchOS Discovery (also called MinistryOS) is a 7-phase engagement framework designed for churches, ministries, and faith-based nonprofits. Every ministry area is assessed across **6 pillars** plus a spiritual foundation layer. Gaps are identified and scored, automation opportunities are ranked, and a master ministry playbook is produced.

**Duration:** 6-12 weeks depending on org size
**Deliverables:** Ministry area playbooks, master church playbook, automation roadmap, KPI scorecards
**Spiritual Foundation:** Every phase begins and ends with prayer alignment and mission-centering

---

## The 6 Pillars + Prayer Foundation

Every ministry area is assessed against these pillars:

| Pillar | Focus | Key Questions |
|--------|-------|---------------|
| **Prayer** | Spiritual foundation, discernment, alignment | Is this ministry bathed in prayer? Is the team spiritually aligned? |
| **Vision** | Mission alignment, ministry goals, success definition | Does this ministry know its purpose and how it serves the church's mission? |
| **People** | Volunteer/staff roles, job descriptions, onboarding, retention | Does everyone know what they own? Are volunteers equipped and appreciated? |
| **Data** | Attendance, engagement, giving metrics, ministry health indicators | What numbers tell you if this ministry is healthy and growing? |
| **Processes** | Sunday workflows, event planning, follow-up, check-in, scheduling | How does ministry work actually get done week-to-week? |
| **Meetings** | Team huddles, planning retreats, cross-ministry coordination | How does information flow? Where do things fall through the cracks? |
| **Issues** | Volunteer burnout, safety concerns, communication gaps, recurring problems | What's broken, frustrating, or draining energy from the mission? |

---

## Pillar Maturity Scoring Rubric

Each pillar is scored 1-5 per ministry area:

| Score | Level | Description |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| **1** | Non-Existent | Nothing exists. No documentation, no tracking, no structure. |
| **2** | Ad-Hoc | Informal. Lives in someone's head. Done differently each time. |
| **3** | Partial | Partially documented. Inconsistently followed. Some people know, some don't. |
| **4** | Documented | Written down. Mostly followed. Some gaps in execution or review. |
| **5** | Optimized | Fully documented, consistently executed, regularly reviewed and improved. |

### Church-Specific Scoring Guidelines

**Prayer (1-5):**
- 1: No prayer structure around ministry decisions or operations
- 2: Prayer happens informally, not integrated into planning
- 3: Prayer at meetings but not consistent, no prayer team for this ministry
- 4: Regular prayer rhythm, prayer team supports ministry, spiritual alignment check-ins
- 5: Prayer is foundational to all decisions, team is spiritually aligned, regular discernment

**Vision (1-5):**
- 1: Ministry exists by tradition, no stated purpose or goals
- 2: Informal sense of purpose but nothing written, no measurable goals
- 3: Purpose statement exists but isn't communicated or tracked
- 4: Clear purpose tied to church mission, annual goals, tracked quarterly
- 5: Vision cascades from church mission, measured regularly, adapted as needed

**People (1-5):**
- 1: No volunteer descriptions, no accountability, no onboarding
- 2: Informal roles, people just "show up and help," high turnover
- 3: Some role descriptions, inconsistent onboarding, no appreciation structure
- 4: Written role descriptions, onboarding process, volunteer appreciation plan
- 5: Clear roles with accountability, development paths, succession plans, retention strategy

**Data (1-5):**
- 1: No metrics tracked, no idea if ministry is healthy or growing
- 2: Headcounts at events, maybe giving totals, reviewed rarely
- 3: Some metrics in ChMS but not consistently reviewed or acted on
- 4: Dashboard with key metrics (attendance, engagement, volunteers), reviewed monthly
- 5: Real-time ministry health indicators, proactive alerts, data informs decisions

**Processes (1-5):**
- 1: No SOPs, every Sunday is different, no checklists
- 2: Key people know the flow but nothing is written, chaos when they're absent
- 3: Some checklists exist (setup, teardown), inconsistently used
- 4: Core processes documented (Sunday flow, events, check-in), mostly followed
- 5: Full process library, volunteer scheduling automated, handoffs smooth, regularly improved

**Meetings (1-5):**
- 1: No regular team meetings, communication is random texts and hallway conversations
- 2: Meetings happen but no agenda, no follow-up, people feel disconnected
- 3: Weekly huddles exist but no standard format, some things fall through cracks
- 4: Consistent meeting rhythm (weekly/monthly/quarterly), agendas, action items tracked
- 5: Optimized cadence, cross-ministry sync, volunteer leaders included, decisions documented

**Issues (1-5):**
- 1: Problems ignored, volunteer burnout high, safety concerns unaddressed
- 2: Issues discussed casually but no tracking, same problems recur
- 3: Some issues tracked, but root causes not addressed, volunteer morale mixed
- 4: Issue tracking exists, safety protocols in place, most get resolved
- 5: Structured issue resolution, proactive safety measures, volunteer well-being prioritized

## The 7 Phases

### Phase 0: Prayer & Commitment
**Goal:** Establish spiritual foundation and secure leadership buy-in.

**Tasks:**
- [ ] Establish prayer team for the discovery process
- [ ] Set prayer schedule & rhythm
- [ ] Define spiritual goals for the process
- [ ] Secure leadership buy-in & commitment

**Output:** Prayer team active, spiritual alignment established, leadership committed

---

### Phase 1: Vision Alignment
**Goal:** Ensure the church's mission, vision, and values are clear, current, and shared.

**Tasks:**
- [ ] Review current mission statement
- [ ] Conduct vision alignment workshop
- [ ] Draft updated vision document
- [ ] Communicate vision to the organization

**Output:** Updated vision document, leadership aligned, congregation informed

---

### Phase 2: Leadership Assessment
**Goal:** Map pastoral and volunteer leadership, assess gifts, identify gaps.

**Tasks:**
- [ ] Inventory current leaders & roles (pastoral staff, elders, ministry leads, volunteers)
- [ ] Assess leadership gifts & strengths
- [ ] Identify leadership gaps
- [ ] Create leadership development plan

**Output:** Leadership assessment, gifts inventory, development plan

---

### Phase 3: Department/Ministry Discovery
**Goal:** 7-layer analysis of every ministry area.

**Discovery Layers:** Prayer, Vision, People, Data, Process, Meetings, Issues

**Common Ministry Areas:**
- Operations
- Communications & Marketing
- Finance & Stewardship
- Volunteer Management
- Donor Relations
- Programs & Ministry (Kids, Youth, Adults, Worship, Missions, Small Groups)

**Tasks:**
- [ ] List all departments / ministries
- [ ] Assign department discovery leads
- [ ] Run discovery sessions per department
- [ ] Document department findings
- [ ] Identify cross-department issues

**Output:** Per-ministry 7-layer assessment, cross-ministry issue register

---

### Phase 4: Gap Analysis & Prioritization
**Goal:** Compile all findings, score gaps, prioritize by mission impact.

**Tasks:**
- [ ] Compile all discovery data
- [ ] Identify critical gaps
- [ ] Prioritize gaps by impact & urgency
- [ ] Present findings to leadership

**Output:** Gap analysis matrix, priority rankings, leadership presentation

---

### Phase 5: Roadmap Creation
**Goal:** Build the implementation plan with quick wins and long-term milestones.

**Tasks:**
- [ ] Define quick wins (30-day actions)
- [ ] Plan medium-term goals (90 days)
- [ ] Set long-term vision milestones
- [ ] Assign owners for each roadmap item

**Output:** Quarterly roadmap, assigned owners, milestone dates

---

### Phase 6: Equip, Empower, Release
**Goal:** Transfer knowledge, equip leaders, ensure sustainability.

**Tasks:**
- [ ] Develop training materials
- [ ] Conduct equipping sessions
- [ ] Delegate authority & decision rights
- [ ] Schedule follow-up reviews

**Output:** Training materials, delegation framework, review cadence, independence readiness

## Support Sections (Run Parallel)

These sections run alongside the main phases:

### S1: MinistryOS Components
The 6 pillars implemented as operational systems:
- [ ] Vision — Mission, Values, Goals documented and communicated
- [ ] People — Org chart, Job Descriptions, Accountability chart
- [ ] Data — KPIs, Scorecard, Ministry health dashboard
- [ ] Process — Playbooks, SOPs, Triggers, Checklists
- [ ] Meetings — Rhythms, Channels, Agendas, Follow-up
- [ ] Issues — Tracking system, Root cause analysis, Resolution log

### S2: Tool Stack Audit
- [ ] Current tools inventory (ChMS, email, social, giving, website)
- [ ] Integration map (what connects to what)
- [ ] Redundancy analysis (duplicate tools, overlapping functions)
- [ ] Recommended stack (consolidation opportunities)
- [ ] Migration plans
- [ ] Vendor evaluation

### S3: Change Management
- [ ] Communication plan (how we tell the congregation/team)
- [ ] Resistance mapping (who might push back, why, how to address)
- [ ] Transition support
- [ ] Staff/volunteer training schedule
- [ ] Celebration milestones (mark progress, acknowledge effort)

### S4: ROI & Impact Tracking
- [ ] Hours saved tracking
- [ ] Cost reduction analysis
- [ ] Mission impact metrics (spiritual growth, community reach, engagement)
- [ ] Board reporting
- [ ] Before & after comparisons

## Automation Opportunity Scoring

Same scoring framework as BusinessOS, adapted for ministry context:

| Dimension | Scale | What It Measures |
|-----------|-------|-----------------|
| **Feasibility** | 1-5 | How practical is this to implement? (volunteer capacity, tech readiness, budget) |
| **Impact** | 1-5 | How much ministry time does this free up? (volunteer hours, admin burden, errors) |
| **Priority** | Calculated | (Feasibility + Impact) × mission alignment weight |

### Common Church Automation Opportunities
- Guest follow-up email sequences (automatic after visit)
- Volunteer scheduling and reminders
- Giving statement generation and delivery
- Event registration and communication
- Check-in system automation (kids/youth)
- Sermon upload and distribution (YouTube, podcast)
- Small group sign-up and leader communication
- Social media post scheduling
- Prayer request routing and follow-up
- New member assimilation pathway tracking

## Ministry Playbook Structure

Each ministry area produces an 8-section standardized playbook:

1. **Ministry Overview** — Purpose, mission alignment, how it serves the church
2. **Organizational Chart & Roles** — Team structure, volunteer descriptions, accountability
3. **Key Metrics & Scorecard** — Ministry health indicators, targets, review frequency
4. **Meeting Rhythms & Communication** — Team meetings, cross-ministry sync, tools
5. **Core Processes & SOPs** — Sunday flow, event planning, volunteer scheduling, follow-up
6. **Known Issues & Resolution Plan** — Pain points, safety concerns, volunteer burnout
7. **Automation Opportunities** — Ranked by mission impact and feasibility
8. **Action Plan & Quick Wins** — Prioritized immediate actions

## Ministry-Specific Discovery Add-Ons

### Worship & Production
- Current worship planning process (Planning Center Services, spreadsheet, verbal)
- Volunteer scheduling for services
- AV/production systems inventory
- Song database / set list archive
- Rehearsal coordination

### Kids & Youth Ministry
- Check-in system (KidCheck, PCO Check-Ins, paper)
- Child protection / background check process
- Parent communication methods
- Curriculum selection and tracking
- Age-group organization

### Small Groups / Life Groups
- Group organization and tracking
- Sign-up process
- Leader recruitment, training, support
- Reporting (attendance, engagement, leader health)
- Group lifecycle (launch, restructure, close)

### Giving & Stewardship
- Giving platform (Tithe.ly, Pushpay, PCO Giving)
- Giving reconciliation with accounting
- Automatic giving statements
- Stewardship campaigns / annual pledge
- First-time giver follow-up

### Guest Follow-Up
- Guest identification method (connect card, digital form, verbal)
- Follow-up process (email, text, call, visit)
- Response time SLA
- Guest → Regular → Member → Serving pathway
- Journey tracking and documentation

## Master Church Playbook

All ministry playbooks are compiled into a single **Master Church Playbook** that includes:

- Table of contents
- All ministry area playbook sections
- Organization-wide automation opportunities (aggregated and ranked)
- Implementation roadmap
- ROI projections and mission impact metrics

This becomes the **single source of truth** for how the church operates.
